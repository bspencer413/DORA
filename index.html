About Memory Watch

Three brains. One mission. Memory Watch.

Memory Watch was produced by three brains — mine, ChatGPT, and Claude (Anthropic). I'm a retired 74 year old former high-tech entrepreneur and early adopter of new tech. I've owned just about every Apple product that has come out since the Apple II. I love the evolution of search tools and have become enamored with AI and how to use it for useful things. 23andMe, I'm in. Ancestry, I'm in. AI, count me ecstatic and in.

As I get older, traditional search engines have become really frustrating, so I find asking AI to be much more effective. However, one thing you cannot do until now is to have a "watchlist" of some kind that can alert you to events that are important to you. In my case I found it very hard, using existing tools, to find out if former friends, classmates, colleagues and even famous figures were dead or alive. Hence, Memory Watch.

We have partnered with Legacy, the largest obituary platform in the United States, covering more than 70% of all U.S. deaths through partnerships with thousands of newspapers and funeral homes nationwide. Memory Watch monitors Legacy continuously, so when someone on your watchlist passes away, you'll know.

Memory Watch is also partnered with Wikipedia which provides biography information on famous or notable people. The intelligent search and detection system powering our ALIVE/DECEASED engine was developed with Claude by Anthropic. A portion of proceeds will be donated to Wikipedia to help assure their continued great service.

Finally, we want to make this the most usable and helpful way to keep track of people that matter to you. If you have any thoughts on how to improve Memory Watch, please contact us with your suggestions.
